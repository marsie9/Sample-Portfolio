About Me

I am an artist dedicated to exploring and expressing human rights in today's world through a diverse range of mediums. My work spans sculptures, compositions, and other creative forms, each designed to provoke thought and spark conversations about the social issues shaping our time. Through art, I aim to challenge perspectives, amplify underrepresented voices, and inspire action, believing that creativity is a powerful catalyst for change and justice.

Most Recent Projects

The Voice Unchained

This project explores the concept of freedom of speech as a cornerstone of human rights and democracy. The work features fragmented yet interconnected elements, such as chained mouths breaking free, abstract sound waves transforming into shapes, and muted colors evolving into vibrant hues. The goal is to capture the courage it takes to speak out, the barriers that attempt to silence voices, and the resilience of those who persist. By engaging with this project, I hope to encourage viewers to reflect on their own rights, the power of their voice, and the importance of preserving freedom of expression globally.

Balancing The Scales

This project delves into the universal quest for equality, emphasizing the importance of balance, fairness, and mutual respect. Central elements include figures of varying shapes and sizes, all standing on an intricately balanced platform, symbolizing unity and shared humanity. The compositions incorporate soft gradients merging different colors and textures, representing the beauty of diversity coming together harmoniously. By visually exploring themes of inequality, resilience, and the strength of community, the project seeks to inspire conversations about bridging divides and creating a world where everyone stands on equal ground.

The Weight of Justice

This project examines the concept of justice and the delicate balance required to uphold fairness in an unequal world. Through a series of powerful sculptures and visual compositions, I explore themes of accountability, truth, and equity. The compositions feature bold contrasts—sharp lines versus fluid shapes, dark tones blending into bursts of light—highlighting the tension between oppression and liberation. By engaging with these works, viewers are invited to reflect on their own role in shaping a world where justice is not just an ideal, but a reality for all.
